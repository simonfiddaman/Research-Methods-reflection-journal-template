## original table

| Criteria | Ratings ||||
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Task 1 Considering the area of interest and the pre-defined Aim, formulate 3 research questions and justify your decisions. | **Proficient** Research question(s) are clearly identified. Decisions for the formulated research question are clearly justified and supported | **Generally sound** The formulated research question(s) are somewhat identified and discussed. The decisions for the formulated research question(s) are somewhat justified. | **Developing** Limited attempt to identify and discuss the formulated research question(s). Limited attempt to justify the decisions for the formulated research question (s). | **Deficient** Inaccurate or inadequate attempt to identify and discuss the formulated research question(s). Inaccurate or inadequate attempt to justify the decisions for the formulated research question(s). |
| Task 2a: identify and critically evaluate suitable research methodologies and methods to address the research question. consider the nature of the relationship between the research question, methodology, and method. | **Proficient** The research methodologies and methods are clearly stated. Competent discussion on both methodologies and methods. Competent comparison showing sustained critical reasoning which reflects competent analysis, very well supported by evidence. | **Generally sound** The research methodologies and methods are stated to some degree. Adequate discussion on both methodologies and methods. Adequate comparison showing emerging critical reasoning which reflects acceptable analysis, somewhat supported by evidence | **Developing** Limited or inaccurate discussion on both methodologies and methods. Limited supporting evidence. One element (methodology, or methods) missing and/or the following limitations: Failure to critically evaluate appropriate methodology and/or failure to explain and justify methodology and/or method | **Deficient** Inaccurate or inadequate attempt to critically evaluate both methodologies and methods. Little supporting evidence. Inaccurate or inadequate methodology and/or methods. |
| Task 2b: Choose Two Research Methodologies: Compare one qualitative and one quantitative approach OR two different qualitative/quantitative methodologies (e.g., case study vs. survey). | **Proficient** Both research methodologies are identified and compared. Competent discussion on both methodologies. Competent comparison showing sustained critical reasoning which reflects competent analysis, very well supported by evidence. | **Generally sound** The research methodologies are compared to some degree. Adequate comparison on both methodologies. Adequate comparison showing critical reasoning which reflects acceptable analysis, somewhat supported by evidence | **Developing** Limited or inaccurate comparison of the research methodologies. Weak and inconclusive comparison. Little supporting evidence. | **Deficient** Inaccurate or inadequate comparison on the research methodologies. Weak and inconclusive comparison. Inadequate supporting evidence. |
| Task 2c: Discuss the Strengths and Limitations of Each methodology: Evaluate the appropriateness of each methodology based on the research question and practical considerations. | **Proficient** The Strengths and Limitations of Each methodology are clearly discussed: There is a competent evaluation on the appropriateness of each methodology based on the research question and practical considerations. very well supported by evidence. | **Generally sound** Adequate discussion on the strengths and limitations of each methodology. Showing adequate evaluation of the appropriateness of each methodology based on the research question and practical considerations. somewhat supported by evidence | **Developing** Limited attempt to identify examples of qualitative and quantitative research methods. Minimal or inaccurate discussion Strengths and Limitations of the research methods identified in relation to addressing the appropriateness of each methodology based on the research question and practical considerations. little use of relevant literature. | **Deficient** Inaccurate or inadequate identified examples of qualitative and quantitative research methods. Inaccurate or inadequate discussion on the Strengths Limitations and appropriateness of each methodology identified based on the research question and practical considerations. with little use of relevant literature. |
| Adherence to academic best practice, including referencing, appropriate and accurate language. | **Proficient** The formatting is as stipulated. The work is appropriately structured, the lines of argument clear and well supported by the evidence. A reasonably clear and relevant line of argument is presented and the relationship of the argument to the wider field of computing knowledge is made explicit and evaluated in a way which is sensitive to the competing claims of different views but offers a conclusion which is defensible and justifiable. Well presented with appropriate referencing and citation details. The references used show competent efforts to research and embed in the paper up-to-date peer-reviewed knowledge. Diagrams, tables and images, if relevant, are mostly effective and appropriately presented and referenced within the body of the report. Where they have been used they support the presented information. The captions are present. | **Generally sound** Adequate in terms of structure, comprehensibility, and quality of presentation. A relevant line of argument is presented and the relationship of this to the wider field of computing knowledge is made explicit. Appropriate referencing and citation details. The references used show acceptable efforts to research and embed in the paper up-to-date peer-reviewed knowledge. Diagrams, tables and images, if relevant, are fairly appropriately presented and referenced within the body of the report. Where they have been used they support the presented information. The captions are present. | **Developing**  The formatting is somewhat as stipulated. Limited in terms of structure, comprehensibility [clear, concise and orderly], and presentation. Limited attempt to develop a line of argument or to link ideas to the wider field of computing knowledge. Limited referencing and citation details. Limited effort to research and embed in the paper up-to-date peer-reviewed knowledge. Diagrams, tables and images, if relevant, are somewhat presented and referenced within the body of the report. Where they have been used they somewhat support the presented information. The captions are limited. | **Deficient** The formatting is not as stipulated. The report is not well organised or structured and the writing style is unclear and inconsistent. Citations are not correct or absent, and not applied in a consistent style. Inaccurate or inadequate attempt to develop a line of argument or to link ideas to the wider field of computing knowledge. Limited referencing and citation details. Inadequate effort to research and embed in the paper up-to-date peer-reviewed knowledge. Diagrams, tables and images, if relevant, are not appropriately presented and referenced within the body of the report. Where they have been used they fail to support the presented information. The captions are missing. |
